# Cyborg Skills Game for Kids Specification

## Purpose

Create an educational game that:
1. Introduces cyborg habits to younger audiences
2. Makes AI collaboration feel natural and fun
3. Prepares the next generation for human-AI partnership
4. Creates a consumer product for families
5. Extends brand into education market

## Target Audience

### Primary: Children Ages 8-14
- **8-10**: Simple habit introduction, guided gameplay
- **11-14**: More complex scenarios, independent play
- **Focus**: Making AI collaboration as natural as using any tool

### Secondary: Parents & Educators
- Parents looking for productive screen time
- Teachers seeking AI literacy resources
- Homeschool families
- After-school programs

## Game Concept

### Core Idea
A game where players complete challenges by effectively collaborating with an AI companion, learning the 7 habits through adventure and problem-solving.

### Title Options
- "Cyborg Academy"
- "AI Quest"
- "The Cyborg Team"
- "Future Skills Adventure"

### Genre
**Educational Adventure / Puzzle Game**
- Story-driven progression
- Challenge-based levels
- Achievement system
- Character customization

## Game Mechanics

### The AI Companion
- Player has an AI helper character
- Must learn to communicate effectively with AI
- AI responds differently based on how player asks
- Models real AI interaction in kid-friendly way

### The 7 Habits as Game Mechanics

**Habit 1: Explain It**
- Ask AI companion to explain puzzle clues
- Request different explanation levels (simpler/more detail)
- Challenge: Understand concept to solve puzzle

**Habit 2: Plan It**
- Work with AI to create plans for quests
- Break big goals into smaller steps
- Challenge: Complete multi-step missions

**Habit 3: Critique It**
- Ask AI to find problems with player's plans
- Identify what could go wrong
- Challenge: Spot and fix flaws before proceeding

**Habit 4: Guide Me**
- Request step-by-step help for new skills
- Learn game mechanics through AI guidance
- Challenge: Master new abilities

**Habit 5: Imagine It**
- Explore "what if" scenarios with AI
- Creative problem-solving
- Challenge: Envision solutions before trying

**Habit 6: What If**
- Test consequences of decisions
- Explore alternative paths
- Challenge: Predict outcomes

**Habit 7: Improve It**
- Ask AI to help refine solutions
- Iterate on approaches
- Challenge: Optimize performance

### Progression System
- **Levels**: Themed worlds, each emphasizing different habits
- **Stars/Achievements**: Reward effective AI collaboration
- **Character Growth**: Unlock abilities by mastering habits
- **Story Arc**: Save the world by becoming an effective cyborg team

## Visual Style

### Art Direction
- **Style**: Colorful, friendly, slightly futuristic
- **Characters**: Diverse, relatable kid protagonists
- **AI Companion**: Friendly robot/digital creature (not scary)
- **Worlds**: Imaginative environments (space, underwater, jungle tech)

### Asset Production
- **Nano Banana**: Generate character designs, backgrounds, objects
- **Sprite Sheets**: Animations for characters
- **UI Elements**: Kid-friendly interface design

## Platform Options

### Option A: Web-Based Game
**Pros**:
- No app store approval needed
- Easy updates
- Works on any device with browser
- Lower development cost

**Cons**:
- Less immersive
- Requires internet
- Limited device features

### Option B: Mobile App (iOS/Android)
**Pros**:
- More polished experience
- Offline capability
- Better performance
- App store discoverability

**Cons**:
- Higher development cost
- App store approval process
- Separate iOS/Android development

### Option C: Hybrid (PWA)
**Pros**:
- Best of both worlds
- Install-able on devices
- Works offline once loaded
- Single codebase

**Recommended**: Start with **Web-Based** for MVP, consider mobile later.

## Appendix: Sample Gameplay

### Level Example: "The Mysterious Message" (World 1)

**Setup**: Player finds an ancient scroll with strange symbols.

**Challenge**: Understand what the message says.

**Gameplay**:
1. Player approaches scroll, can't read it
2. Prompt to ask AI companion for help
3. First attempt: "What does it say?"
   - AI: "I see symbols, but I need more context. What should I focus on?"
4. Better attempt: "Explain what these symbols might mean"
   - AI provides some insight but it's complex
5. Best attempt: "Explain this like I'm a young explorer who's never seen these before"
   - AI gives clear, kid-friendly explanation
6. Player now understands and can solve the puzzle

**Learning**: How to ask for explanations at the right level.

**Reward**: Star for effective communication, progress to next level.
