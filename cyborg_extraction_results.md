## 1. CYBORG HABITS - COMPLETE OVERVIEW

### Definition and Core Philosophy

**Cyborg Habits** is a behavior-change program designed as "Level Zero" of the broader Cyborg Skills ecosystem. As stated in the transcripts: *"cyborg habits is level zero which is why I'm not calling it you that you still don't yet have cyborg skills. You have none."*

The program is fundamentally about **behavior change, not learning**: *"The program is intentionally designed not as knowledge transfer exercise, but as a behavior change initiative focused on creating experiences that lead to lasting changes in how people work with AI."*

The core philosophy centers on making AI into **"transparent equipment"** - technology that becomes invisible to the user: *"When you use you're good at using a pencil or a musical instrument... you don't think about the pencil. You just think about your intention and it coming into the world."*

### Program Structure and Implementation

#### Daily Challenge Framework
- **Duration**: 15-17 day program (originally 15, expanded to 17)
- **Time Commitment**: 4-10 minutes per day "in the flow of work"
- **Structure**: Two-part daily challenges combining different habits
- **Format**: Two videos + two challenges + reflection question per day
- **Completion Threshold**: 65-75% of challenges for program completion
- **Self-paced**: Strategic nudges while preserving user autonomy

#### Seven Core Habits Framework
The program teaches seven fundamental habits, described as *"the seven habits of highly effective cyborgs"* (referencing Stephen Covey's framework):

1. **Explain it** - Get AI to explain complex concepts simply
2. **Plan it** - Create detailed multi-step strategies
3. **Critique it** - Challenge assumptions, spot risks, uncover blind spots
4. **Guide it/Guide me** - Navigate new challenges and unfamiliar territory
5. **Imagine it** - Explore possibilities and test scenarios
6. **What if** - Ask hypothetical questions and explore implications
7. **Improve it** - Enhance and refine outputs and ideas

### Platform Features and User Experience

#### Technical Infrastructure
- **Database Integration**: Superbase backend with row-level security
- **Progress Tracking**: "Evidence map" (renamed from "progress map") tracking habit formation
- **Automated Email System**: Bi-weekly updates, welcome sequences, completion notifications
- **Video Platform**: YouTube integration with subtitle editing capabilities
- **User Interface**: Netflix-style interface with challenge tiles

#### User Journey Design
- **Pre-program**: Survey 2 days before first challenge
- **Day 1**: Introductory video and baseline assessment
- **Daily Flow**: Email invitation → platform access → complete challenges → mark complete → optional reflection
- **Post-program**: Follow-up survey measuring habit adoption frequency
- **Challenge Cards**: Physical/digital representations of daily activities

### Results and Effectiveness

- **High Relevance**: "Almost nine out of 10 participants rated the habits as relevant on highly relevant regardless of organizational context"
- **Most Valuable Habits**: "Improve it and critique it showed up again... participants rely on them for raising quality, identifying gaps, structuring thinking, and sharpening outputs"
- **Cognitive Improvements**: "22 out of 31 high improvement total. So, Pragma strongly shows strong gains in cognitive approach, not just quality"
- **Completion Rate**: 75% individual completion rate threshold

#### User Challenges
- **Technical Friction**: "Technical friction loss of prompts or chat history was a significant frustration and broke continuity for many participants"
- **Workload Pressure**: "At least 16 participants refer to workload or pressure as an obstacle"
- **Harder Habits**: "Plan it and imagine it are harder to activate in real world conditions"
